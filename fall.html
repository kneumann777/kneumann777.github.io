Fall in Alaska

Fall in Alaska brings a slowing of tourism season. September is known for being hit-or-miss: sometimes it's a month of beautiful late summer, and sometimes it brings rain and cooler weather. The foliage begins to change, and with the sun going down earlier, the Northern Lights make their reappearance. Most activities available in the summer are also available in the fall, but are often cheaper/less busy. Notably, fall only lasts for a couple of months, ending in early September in the northern part of Alaska.
