Winter in Alaska

Winter in Alaska comes early and stays for a while. The sun goes down earlier, and stays down for longer. The northern part of Alaska will only get 4 hours of sunlight in the depth of winter. Temperatures drop and snow covers the entire region. Despite all this, it doesn't mean the entirety of Alaska shuts down. There are numerous ski hills to visit, Northern Lights to see, and several festivals to partake in, including the famous Iditarod Sled Dog race.
